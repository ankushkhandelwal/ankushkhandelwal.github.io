Projects

AI assisted geo-tagging of news articles using Large Language Models

  • GOAL: Extract geographic location of flood events from news articles, and create a two-sentence summary of the event.
  • CHALLENGE: Not all locations mentioned in an article refer to a flooded location, and traditional Named Entity Recognition approach will not be sufficient.
  • SOLUTION: Generate a task-aware prompt by asking LLM general questions and using response keywords to guide prompt creation.
  • IMPACT: Achieved 70% reduction in human annotator time for evaluating 20K image captions from 9K news articles, enabling rapid detection of events like flash floods which are often not monitored via satellite imagery.

Efficient semantic segmentation at scale

  • GOAL: build models that are robust at segmenting satellite imagery at large scale.
  • CHALLENGE: No access to ground truth, and label annotation is time consuming.
  • SOLUTION: Modeled reconstruction as a linear unmixing task using a very light-weight MLP only decoder, which leads to significant reduction in model parameters. Simultaneous training of segmentation decoder (with minimal labeled data) and reconstruction decoder (with large unlabeled) allows robust semantic segmentation with minimal effort.
  • IMPACT: The approach is being used to map all Arctic lakes, finally tackling the challenge of unknown smaller lakes hindering accurate methane emission estimates. This work will significantly improve our understanding of the Arctic's role in global methane emissions.

Ranking using Noisy and Incomplete Binary Ratings

  • GOAL: Improve accuracy of water extent maps available from the state-of-the-art dataset (GSW dataset).
  • CHALLENGE: GSW water maps contain lot of missing data and labelling errors.
  • SOLUTION: Proposed a new approach to rank pixels by leveraging the physical principles governing lake area changes.
  • IMPACT: Created the first global product that provides surface area variations of more than 0.5 million water bodies.

Entity Aware Learning

  • GOAL: Create robust predictive models for related entities.
  • CHALLENGE: Very limited training samples for each entity, and external knowledge about entities are often not available or incomplete.
  • SOLUTION: In scenarios where entities during testing phase were available during training phase, assigning random entity characteristics can enable robust learning compared to building a single model by combining data from all entities. Furthermore, entities characteristics can be learned using input/output data by leveraging a novel contrastive loss function.
  • IMPACT: Showed how robust personalized models can be learned for each entity without needing to collect external information about them.

Physics Guided Machine Learning for Streamflow Prediction

  • GOAL: Improve prediction of streamflow from weather drivers.
  • CHALLENGE: The relationship between weather and streamflow in complex and heterogeneous. For e.g., same amount of rainfall can lead to very different streamflow depending on amount of moisture already present in the soil.
  • SOLUTION: Proposed a multi-task learning framework to model not only streamflow but also state variables such as soil moisture, leaf area index, snowpack. This allows the model to indirectly learn complex relationship between system states and its response (streamflow).
  • IMPACT: Significant reduction in prediction errors of streamflow compared to modeling only streamflow. While the current study was conducted on synthetic dataset from a physics based model, all the state variables considered in this work are available at scale from different sources and hence this work has the potential to improve performance in operational settings.
  • Khandelwal, A., Xu, S., Li, X., Jia, X., Stienbach, M., Duffy, C., Nieber, J. and Kumar, V., 2020. Physics guided machine learning methods for hydrology. arXiv preprint arXiv:2012.02854.

Ensemble Learning using Error Correcting Output Coding

  • GOAL: Create global surface water extent maps using satellite imagery.
  • CHALLENGE: Tremendous heterogeneity in how land and water appear in satellite imagery.
  • SOLUTION: Proposed a new ensemble learning approach for binary classification that takes into account bi-partite nature of modalities in the data.
  • IMPACT: Created a 15 year history surface water at global scale, and found many interesting patterns which were previously not explored in detail including sudden rise of areal extents of lakes in the Tibetan plateau after 2000, negative correlation between ground water and surface water in Asia due to water intensive crops such as rice.

Learning from Imperfect Annotations

  • GOAL: Create maps of annual increase in palm oil plantations using satellite imagery.
  • CHALLENGE: Existing plantation maps provided by different organizations have either poor precision or recall.
  • SOLUTION: Used a generative model which capture the relationship between the latent true label and the imperfect labels from multiple annotators.
  • IMPACT: Created 15 year history of conversion of forest to palm oil in plantations in Indonesia.
  • Jia, X., Khandelwal, A., Gerber, J., Carlson, K., West, P. and Kumar, V., 2016, December. Learning large-scale plantation mapping from imperfect annotators. In 2016 IEEE international conference on big data (big data) (pp. 1192-1201). IEEE.

Improving Label Quality using an ensemble of one-class models

  • GOAL: Create a high accuracy map of cover crops in mid-west. Cover crops are crops grown after the primary crop is harvested.
  • CHALLENGE: Even small amount of errors in large primary crops such and corn and soybean can be a major issue in mapping cover crops (e.g. even 2 % error in corn/Soybean can lead to more than 50 % error of commission for Cover crop Mapping )
  • SOLUTION: Used an ensemble of multiple one-class SVMs trained for noisy crops labels from USDA. USDA labels were updated based on accept/reject patterns by different one-class models.
  • IMPACT: Significantly reduced errors of commission on green ditches at the edges of crop fields labeled as Corn/Soybeans by USDA due to mixed pixel issues at 30m spatial resolution.
  • Jia, X., Khandelwal, A., Mulla, D.J., Pardey, P.G. and Kumar, V., 2019. Bringing automated, remote‐sensed, machine learning methods to monitoring crop landscapes at scale. Agricultural Economics, 50, pp.41-50.

Domain Aware Label Refinement using Hidden Markov Models

  • GOAL: Create robust maps of annual changes in impervious area using satellite imagery.
  • CHALLENGE: Annual changes are often significantly smaller than classification errors.
  • SOLUTION: Used probabilities of transitions using Hidden Markov models to remove spurious changes. For example, probability of a vegetation pixel converting to impervious is higher than vice-versa.
  • IMPACT: Used the approach to create 10 year urbanization history for 7 cities in Brazil.
  • Mithal, V., Khandelwal, A., Boriah, S., Steinhaeuser, K. and Kumar, V., 2013, May. Change detection from temporal sequences of class labels: Application to land cover change mapping. In Proceedings of the 2013 SIAM International Conference on Data Mining (pp. 650-658). Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics.
