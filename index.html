Projects

AI assisted geo-tagging of news articles using Large Language Models

  • GOAL: Build a catalog of flood events by extracting geographic locations from news articles and create a two-sentence summary for each event.
  • CHALLENGE: Not all locations mentioned in an article refer to a flooded area, making the traditional Named Entity Recognition (NER) approach insufficient.
  • SOLUTION: Generate task-aware prompts by asking Large Language Models (LLMs) conceptual questions about the task at hand, and using the response keywords to guide the creation of prompts.
  • IMPACT: Achieved a 70% reduction in human annotator time by evaluating 20,000 image captions from 9,000 news articles, enabling the rapid detection of events like flash floods, which are often not monitored via satellite imagery.

Efficient semantic segmentation at scale

  • GOAL: Build models that are robust at segmenting satellite imagery at large scale.
  • CHALLENGE: No access to ground truth, and label annotation is time consuming.
  • SOLUTION: Simultaneous training of segmentation decoder (with minimal labeled data) and reconstruction decoder (with large unlabeled) allows robust semantic segmentation with minimal human effort. Labeled data is also generated using an AI-assisted annotation workflow which further reduces annotation cost.
  • IMPACT: The approach is being used to map all Arctic lakes, finally tackling the challenge of unknown smaller lakes hindering accurate methane emission estimates. This work will significantly improve our understanding of the Arctic's role in global methane emissions.
  • Manuscript under preparation.

Entity Aware Learning for Personalized Prediction

  • GOAL: Create robust predictive models for related entities.
  • CHALLENGE: There are very limited training samples for each entity, and external knowledge about the entities is often not available or incomplete.
  • SOLUTION: In scenarios where the entities tested were also present during the training phase, assigning random characteristics to each entity can enable more robust learning compared to building a single model with combined data from all entities. Additionally, entity characteristics can be learned from input/output data by leveraging a novel contrastive loss function for timeseries data.
  • IMPACT: Demonstrated operational improvements in modeling streamflow (flow of water through rivers) from weather timeseries data.

Physics Guided Machine Learning for Streamflow Prediction

  • GOAL: Improve prediction of streamflow from weather drivers.
  • CHALLENGE: The relationship between weather and streamflow in complex and heterogeneous. For e.g., same amount of rainfall can lead to very different streamflow depending on amount of moisture already present in the soil.
  • SOLUTION: Proposed a multi-task learning framework to model not only streamflow but also state variables such as soil moisture, leaf area index, snowpack. This allows the model to indirectly learn complex relationship between system states and its response (streamflow).
  • IMPACT: Significant reduction in prediction errors of streamflow compared to modeling only streamflow. While the current study was conducted on synthetic dataset from a physics based model, all the state variables considered in this work are available at scale from different sources and hence this work has the potential to improve performance in operational settings.

Improving Label Quality using an ensemble of one-class models

  • GOAL: Develop a high-accuracy map of cover crops in the Midwest. Cover crops are those planted following the harvest of the primary crop.
  • CHALLENGE: Minor errors in mapping large primary crops such as corn and soybean can significantly affect the accuracy of cover crop mapping. For example, a 2% error in corn or soybean mapping can result in more than a 50% commission error for cover crop mapping.
  • SOLUTION: Implemented an ensemble of multiple one-class Support Vector Machines (SVMs) trained with noisy crop labels from the USDA. The USDA labels were refined based on acceptance/rejection patterns identified by the different one-class models.
  • IMPACT: Achieved a significant reduction in commission errors for green ditches on the edges of crop fields erroneously labeled as Corn/Soybeans by the USDA, addressing the mixed pixel issues encountered with 30m spatial resolution imagery.
  • Jia, X., Khandelwal, A., Mulla, D.J., Pardey, P.G. and Kumar, V., 2019. Bringing automated, remote‐sensed, machine learning methods to monitoring crop landscapes at scale. Agricultural Economics, 50, pp.41-50.

Domain Aware Label Refinement using Hidden Markov Models

  • GOAL: Develop high-precision maps to track annual changes in impervious surfaces using satellite imagery.
  • CHALLENGE: The annual alterations in impervious areas are frequently much smaller than the errors from classification processes. Hence, even a 95 % accurate model can lead to large errors in amount of impervious area added/removed.
  • SOLUTION: Employed Hidden Markov models to calculate the probabilities of transitions, thereby eliminating spurious changes. For instance, the likelihood of a vegetation pixel transitioning to an impervious state is higher than the reverse.
  • IMPACT: Applied this methodology to document a decade of urbanization across seven cities in Brazil, effectively mapping the expansion of impervious areas.
  • Mithal, V., Khandelwal, A., Boriah, S., Steinhaeuser, K. and Kumar, V., 2013, May. Change detection from temporal sequences of class labels: Application to land cover change mapping. In Proceedings of the 2013 SIAM International Conference on Data Mining (pp. 650-658). Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics.
